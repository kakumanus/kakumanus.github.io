About Me

Like many students, deciding on a major to pursue in college was quite difficult for me. My interests varied immensely - from politics and history to architecture to programming. I finally decided that my love of Computer Science was great enough that it should be the focus of my college career. This decision was supported by my realization that the skills I gain through my Computer Science education could (and should) be applied to a wide range of interdisciplinary problems. My intended academic specialization, Artificial Intelligence, will provide me with the tools to tackle these complex issues.

Aside from a love of programming and technology, I was always fascinated by cities. Everything from their history to their growth, design, and problems intrigued me and as a result, I decided to minor in City and Regional Planning. As with many disciplines, the field of urban design and planning is becoming increasingly reliant on technology. I imagine myself working at this intersection - perhaps helping build systems where transportation can be made 'smarter' and more efficient.
